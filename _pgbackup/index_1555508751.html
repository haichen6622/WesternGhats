Influence of the Boreal Summer Intraseasonal Oscillation on Monsoon Rainfall and Soil Moisture over the Western Ghats, India  

The India summer monsoon plays an essential role in the agriculture and hydroelectric power industry for the Indian subcontinent, but it is also strongly associated with the occurrence of flooding, drought and rain-induced landslides by altering soil moisture. Due to its roles in social-economic activities and extreme hydro-climatic events, understanding the monsoon rainfall and its relationship with soil moisture is of great importance.

Moreover, the activity of Boreal Summer Intraseasonal Oscillation (BSISO) is known to have a strong link with extreme wet or dry conditions for different regions, but few papers focus on the impacts of BSISO on rainfall variability and soil moisture response over India. Owing to the most recent data from two flux towers (Figure 1) on Western Ghats installed by the Centre for Ecology and Hydrology for the Joint project of INCOMPASS (Fletcher et al., 2018), there is a good opportunity to fill this research gap. 

Aims and Objectives

This research project aims to investigate how active phases of BSISO influence the rainfall variability and soil moisture response for summer monsoon seasons (June to September) during 2016 - 2018 over the Western Ghats, India.
